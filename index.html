What is Inflation?

Inflation is the rate of increase in prices over a given period of time. It is typically a broad measure, such as the overall increase in prices or the increase in the cost of living in a country.

Causes of Inflation

When demand for goods and services outpaces the available supply, availability of certain goods and services becomes constrained, resulting in inflationary pressures.

Effects of Inflation

  • The rise in prices over time reduces the purchasing power of consumers since a fixed amount of money will afford progressively less consumption.

  • Inflation tends to raise interest rates as Central banks may adjust rates to control inflation, impacting how much consumers can buy with their money.

Types of inflation

Demand-pull inflation:

Not enough products or services being produced to keep up with demand, causing their prices to increase.

Cost-push inflation:

Cost of producing products and services rises, forcing businesses to raise their prices.

Built-in inflation:

Demand for higher wages causes businesses to raise their prices in order to offset their rising wage costs.
